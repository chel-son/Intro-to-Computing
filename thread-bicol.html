MAIN FOOD

Bicol Express

Bicol Express is a popular Filipino dish from the Bicol region, recognized for its robust and spicy flavors. This substantial stew is made with pork, shrimp, or fish cooked in coconut milk and is generally flavored with long green chilies called "siling haba," as well as chili flakes for an added kick. The thick, creamy coconut milk tempers the heat of the chilies, resulting in a tasty and soothing dish that has become a favorite throughout the Philippines. Bicol Express, which is often served with steamed rice, symbolizes the region's love of coconut and spice, making it a must-try for tourists looking to sample Filipino food. Over the years, numerous varieties of Bicol Express have arisen, with some using beef or even tofu for a vegetarian option.

MAIN FESTIVAL

Peñafrancia Festival

Held every September in Naga City, Bicol, is one of the most significant religious events in the Philippines. It honors the Nuestra Señora de Peñafrancia, the patroness of Bicol, with a grand procession and various cultural activities that draw millions of devotees and tourists. The highlight of the festival is the fluvial procession, where the image of the Virgin is carried along the Bicol River in a colorful and reverent boat procession. The celebration also includes masses, street dancing, and vibrant performances that showcase the deep devotion of the people of Bicol to their patroness, making the festival a powerful expression of faith and culture.

MAIN ATTRACTION

Mt. Mayon

Mayon Volcano, located in the Philippines' Bicol area, is known for its nearly perfect, symmetrical cone shape, making it one of the world's most famous and active volcanoes. The volcano is a major tourist destination, attracting visitors from all over the world who come to observe its spectacular look and participate in activities such as trekking, sightseeing, and photography, particularly while it is not erupting. Mayon is one of the Philippines' most active volcanoes, with the most recent eruptions occurring in the last few years.Standing 2,462 meters (8,077 feet) tall, it serves as a daily reminder of nature's power and beauty.
