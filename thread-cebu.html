FOOD

Lechon Baboy

Cebu's version of lechon is known for its perfect blend of crispy skin and delicate, savory meat. Unlike other regional varieties, Cebu lechon is marinated with a unique blend of spices, including lemongrass, garlic, and ginger, which give the pig a delightful scent. The lechon is roasted over an open flame, turning the skin golden and crispy while keeping the meat juicy inside. Lechon baboy is frequently served during festivals, parties, and family gatherings. The dish is so famous that it has become a must-try delicacy for visitors to the island. Its peculiar flavor and preparation style have gained it recognition as one of the Philippines' most recognized culinary treasures.

FESTIVAL

Peñafrancia Festival

The Sinulog Festival, celebrated on the third Sunday of January in Cebu City, honors the Santo Niño (the Child Jesus). The streets come alive with a beautiful parade of dancers dressed in colorful costumes, marching to the rhythmic beat of drums as they perform the classic Sinulog dance, which depicts the flow of water. Beyond its religious significance, Sinulog is a spectacular celebration of Cebu's rich history and culture. This world-famous festival attracts millions of people each year, bringing a riveting blend of faith, culture, and excitement as locals and tourists come together in a joyous atmosphere of music, cuisine, and dance.

ATTRACTION

Little Kyoto

Also known as the "Japanese Garden," offers a peaceful retreat that transports visitors to a serene, Japanese-inspired atmosphere. Located in the town of Carmen, this unique destination features beautiful gardens, traditional Japanese architecture, and picturesque koi ponds, all set against the backdrop of lush greenery. The area’s design reflects the tranquil beauty of Japan, making it an ideal spot for relaxation, photography, and a taste of Japanese culture. Visitors can explore a variety of features, such as the iconic red torii gate, Zen-inspired landscapes, and a quaint teahouse, all contributing to the calm and inviting ambiance. Little Kyoto has become a popular getaway for both locals and tourists, offering an escape from the hustle and bustle of city life.
