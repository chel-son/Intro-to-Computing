FOOD

Viva Bagnet

A popular treat from Ilocos Sur, Philippines, Vigan bagnet is known for its mouthwateringly rich tastes and addictive crispiness. The skillful cooking of the Ilocano people is demonstrated by this delicacy of deep-fried hog belly. The pig is first air-dried for hours, then cooked with spices to infuse deep, savory tastes, and deep-fried twice for that crackling crunch. It is typically served with fresh tomatoes and accompanied by a side of tart Ilocos vinegar or bagoong.

FESTIVAL

Karbo Festival

A lively and distinctive event where innovation and culture converges in Barbara, Pangasinan. The festival's focal point is the vibrant cows, which represent the town's agricultural past. Street dances and products are accompanied with the use of local materials during the festival. The community honors its farming heritage and celebrates its strong bond with the land and livestock with this vibrant exhibition. The festival also showcases how Karbungko, or water hyacinth, which was formerly thought of as an agricultural annoyance, is now used to make environmentally beneficial products like mats and baskets.

ATTRACTION

Bantay Watchtower

One of Vigan's most recognizable historical sites, also called the Bantay Bell Tower, Constructed in 1591, this building was initially used as a watchtower to protect the town and its residents from pirates and invading forces. Later on, the neighboring church now used it as a bell tower. The tower is perched on a hill and provides scenic views of the surrounding area. Its architecture is composed of red bricks and mixes Gothic elements with Spanish colonial design. It has endured historical upheavals and natural disasters for millennia, serving as a symbol of fortitude and resilience.
